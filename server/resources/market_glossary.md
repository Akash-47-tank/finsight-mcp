# Financial Market Glossary

A reference guide for key terms used in Indian stock market analysis.

---

## Valuation Ratios

**P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)**
The ratio of a company's share price to its earnings per share. A high P/E may indicate the stock is overvalued or that investors expect high future growth. Indian large-caps typically trade at P/E of 15–30x.

**Forward P/E**
P/E calculated using projected (next 12 months) earnings rather than trailing earnings. Useful for growth-stage companies.

**P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book)**
Share price divided by book value per share. A P/B below 1.0 may indicate undervaluation. Banking stocks in India commonly use P/B as a primary metric.

**P/S Ratio (Price-to-Sales)**
Market cap divided by annual revenue. Useful for companies that are not yet profitable.

**EV/EBITDA**
Enterprise Value divided by EBITDA. A more comprehensive valuation metric than P/E as it accounts for debt. Below 10x is generally considered reasonable.

**PEG Ratio**
P/E ratio divided by earnings growth rate. A PEG below 1.0 is often considered undervalued relative to growth.

---

## Profitability Metrics

**EPS (Earnings Per Share)**
Net profit divided by total outstanding shares. Trailing EPS uses past 12 months; Forward EPS uses projections.

**ROE (Return on Equity)**
Net income divided by shareholder equity, expressed as a percentage. Above 15% is considered healthy for Indian companies.

**ROA (Return on Assets)**
Net income divided by total assets. Indicates how efficiently a company uses its assets to generate profit.

**Gross Margin**
(Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue. Higher margins indicate stronger pricing power.

**Operating Margin**
Operating income / Revenue. Excludes interest and taxes.

**Net Profit Margin**
Net income / Revenue. The bottom-line measure of profitability.

---

## Price & Range Metrics

**52-Week High / Low**
The highest and lowest price a stock has traded at over the past 52 weeks. A stock trading near its 52-week high indicates strong momentum.

**52-Week Position %**
Where the current price sits in the 52-week range: 0% = at the low, 100% = at the high.

**50-Day Moving Average (50 DMA)**
Average closing price over the past 50 trading days. Widely used as a short-to-medium term trend indicator.

**200-Day Moving Average (200 DMA)**
Average closing price over the past 200 trading days. Key long-term trend indicator. When price crosses above the 200 DMA, it's called a "Golden Cross" (bullish).

---

## Dividends

**Dividend Yield**
Annual dividend per share / Current stock price, as a percentage. Higher yield can indicate either strong payouts or a falling stock price.

**Payout Ratio**
Percentage of earnings paid out as dividends. A ratio above 80% may be unsustainable.

**Ex-Dividend Date**
You must own the stock before this date to receive the dividend.

---

## Balance Sheet Metrics

**Debt-to-Equity (D/E)**
Total debt / Shareholders' equity. For non-financial companies, below 1.0 is generally healthy. Banks and NBFCs have higher D/E by nature.

**Current Ratio**
Current assets / Current liabilities. Above 1.5 is typically considered healthy. Indicates short-term liquidity.

**Book Value per Share**
(Total Assets - Total Liabilities) / Outstanding Shares. The theoretical value of one share if the company were liquidated.

**Free Cash Flow (FCF)**
Operating cash flow minus capital expenditures. Companies with consistent positive FCF are generally stronger businesses.

---

## Market Terms

**Market Cap**
Total market value of a company's outstanding shares (Price × Shares).
- Large Cap: >₹20,000 Cr
- Mid Cap: ₹5,000–₹20,000 Cr
- Small Cap: <₹5,000 Cr

**NIFTY 50**
India's benchmark index comprising the 50 largest companies on NSE by free-float market cap.

**SENSEX**
BSE's benchmark index comprising 30 large and financially sound companies listed on BSE.

**NSE (National Stock Exchange)**
India's largest stock exchange by trading volume, headquartered in Mumbai.

**BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange)**
Asia's oldest stock exchange; both NSE and BSE list largely the same companies.

**FII / FPI**
Foreign Institutional Investors / Foreign Portfolio Investors. Their buying/selling activity significantly influences Indian market movement.

**DII**
Domestic Institutional Investors — includes mutual funds, LIC, and domestic insurance companies.

**Circuit Breaker**
A regulatory mechanism that halts trading when a stock moves beyond a defined % limit (typically ±5%, ±10%, or ±20%) in a single session.

**F&O (Futures & Options)**
Derivatives segment of NSE. Only NIFTY 50 and select stocks are allowed in F&O trading.
